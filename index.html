Alcohol Consumption Demographics and Outcomes in Portugese Youth

Produced for the final project for Data Visualization (DATA 303) at William & Mary.

By Sarah Larimer & Renee Ritchey

For this project, we wanted to assess risk behavior in teens. We used a dataset looking at alcohol consumption in Portuguese teenagers. We wanted to break down our project into two sections: Who is more likely to engage in elevated drinking habits? And what are the consequences of this high risk behavior?

Going Out

The first question we wanted to ask was to confirm our hypothesis that the more days a week a teenager goes out, the more likely they are to drink. As you can see from this graph below, this is exactly what we see in the data.

Family Traits

The next aspect we wanted to look at for who is more likely to drink is how family dynamics are related to weekend drinking habits. We looked at the distribution of weekend alcohol consumption in relation to whether or not parents are still together and whether or not the student lives in an urban or rural environment. Health

Now that we know who is more likely to drink, the second question we wanted to evaluate is what are the consequences of elevated drinking behaviors. The first question we answered was what are the health effects of high risk behavior. We attempted to demonstrate this through the visualization below.

Grades

Lastly, the other consequential thing we wanted to see through visualization was the impact of high risk behavior on school. Through this, we can see not only how alcohol consumption impacts health outcomes but also educational outcomes.
